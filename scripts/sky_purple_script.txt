Why isn't the sky purple? After all, violet light has the shortest wavelength and scatters more than blue.

Here's the wild truth. The sky actually IS violet. But you can't see it.

Your eyes have three color sensors: red, green, and blue. Violet light triggers blue AND red receptors simultaneously. Your brain interprets this mixture as... blue. Not purple.

Plus, the sun emits less violet light to begin with. And our atmosphere absorbs some of it before it reaches your eyes.

Evolution also played a role. Humans evolved to see the colors most useful for survival. Violet wasn't priority.

So next time someone asks why the sky is blue, tell them it's actually violet. We just can't see it.
