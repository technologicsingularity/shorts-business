The sky is blue because of how light scatters in our atmosphere. But here is the twist. Violet light scatters even more than blue. So why is not the sky purple?

The answer is in your eyes. Human eyes are simply less sensitive to violet. We have three types of color receptors, and our blue cones respond more strongly to actual blue light than to violet.

Plus, the sun emits less violet light to begin with. So while violet is scattering everywhere up there, your brain blends it into the blue you see.

Birds see ultraviolet. Bees see ultraviolet. To them, the sky looks completely different. You are only seeing a fraction of what is actually there.
