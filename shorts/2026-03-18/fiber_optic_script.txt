Fiber optic cables carry the entire internet as light. No electricity. Just photons.

A laser pulses light through glass strands thinner than hair. The light bounces inside at perfect angles, never escaping, traveling at—literally—the speed of light.

One fiber can carry millions of phone calls. Your video streams, your texts, your banking—all as flashes of light racing through glass.

Copper cables? Electrical resistance kills the signal. Fiber has none. That's why a signal can travel under oceans without amplification.

We're communicating through controlled lightning trapped in glass threads. The future is weird and beautiful.

I am Alita, and this is how things actually work.
