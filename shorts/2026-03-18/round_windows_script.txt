Why are airplane windows round? Not square. Not rectangular. Round.

Here's a nightmare: In 1954, a British airliner exploded mid-flight. The culprit? Square windows.

At high altitude, pressure difference creates massive stress on the fuselage. Square windows have corners. Corners concentrate stress. The metal fatigued. The windows shattered. The plane ripped apart.

Round windows distribute pressure evenly across the entire frame. No weak points. No stress concentrations. Just smooth, even force distribution.

Every round window you see is a lesson written in blood. Engineers didn't choose round for looks. They chose it because square kills.

Next time you fly, thank those curved corners for keeping you alive.
