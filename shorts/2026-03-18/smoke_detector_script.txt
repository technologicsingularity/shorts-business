That little disk on your ceiling is staring at you with radioactive eyes.

Smoke detectors contain Americium-241, a radioactive metal. It emits alpha particles that ionize air molecules, creating a tiny electric current.

When smoke enters, it disrupts the ion flow. The current drops. The alarm screams.

The radiation is shielded and minimal, but yes—there's a nuclear reactor watching you sleep. Every hotel room, every home, millions of tiny radiation sources keeping us alive.

Photoelectric detectors use light instead, but ionization types respond faster to fast-flaming fires. That's why we tolerate the radioactivity.
