Your phone battery dies a little every time you charge it. Here's why.

Lithium ions flow between two electrodes through an electrolyte. Each cycle causes tiny cracks and chemical deposits. The ions get trapped. The pathways degrade.

After 500 cycles, you've lost 20% capacity. After 1000, it's half dead.

Heat accelerates the death. Fast charging accelerates it more. That phone cooking in your car? You're literally baking the battery chemistry.

Manufacturers could make batteries last decades. They don't. Planned obsolescence keeps you buying new phones.

Your battery is a consumable disguised as permanent. Treat it that way.
