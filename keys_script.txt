Keys aren't random shapes. They're tiny puzzles.

Every time you turn a key, you're not just pushing metal. You're solving a mechanical lock.

See these jagged edges? Each one pushes a pin inside the lock to a specific height. When all pins align at the shear line, the cylinder turns. One wrong height, and the lock stays frozen.

But here's the twist: master keys. A single key that opens multiple different locks.

How? Extra pin stacks. The lock has two shear lines now. Your regular key hits the first. The master key hits the second. Same lock, different solutions.

Those random shapes? Precision engineering. Every groove calculated. Every angle purposeful.

Next time you unlock a door, remember: you're not just turning metal. You're completing a tiny mechanical puzzle designed centuries ago.
